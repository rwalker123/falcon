Client UX proposal · Band / City dock · rev 2, no-scroll

The Band panel is three panels wearing one coat.

Everything in it today is a flat run of stepper rows — the band's own orders, the workforce's assignments, and the parties that already left camp all share one vertical list and one visual weight. That is why Move looks like it belongs to Warrior, why a policy picker looks like it belongs to the scouting button, and why thirty worked sources would bury all of it. This proposal separates the three, and gives the work list a shape that fits a late-game band without a scrollbar anywhere.

01

What's wrong today

Your four findings, plus two things I found underneath them.

01

The actions list has no ceiling

Each worked source is a three-line row (title + stepper, yield line, arrival strip). Six sources already fill the panel; thirty would be roughly 900px of unbroken list, pushing Band roles and Move off the bottom of the dock entirely. There is no grouping, no subtotal, no sort, and no bound of any kind — the list grows and the panel grows with it.

02

Band roles read as two more actions

Scout and Warrior use the identical stepper row as a Forage or Hunt assignment, under a label that looks the same as CURRENT ACTIONS. But they are a different kind of thing: a standing posture with no source, no tile, no yield and no policy. Same control shape implies same semantics.

03

Move / Clear all sit at the bottom of a list they don't belong to

They are band-level verbs — relocate the whole band, unassign everyone — but they render directly beneath the Warrior stepper, so they read as Warrior's buttons. Proximity is the strongest grouping cue on the panel and it is pointing at the wrong owner. The answer turned out not to be relocating them but deleting both — see §02.

04

The expedition block reads as one control stack, and it isn't

Party, Send scouting expedition, the four policy buttons, and Send hunting expedition are one undifferentiated column. The Party stepper feeds both verbs; the policy row feeds only the second. In reading order, Sustain/Surplus/Market look like modifiers on the scouting button directly above them.

✳

The vanished expeditions are not a bug

Two sections have near-identical names. ACTIVE EXPEDITIONS lists parties in the field and renders only when the band has some; SEND EXPEDITION is the launcher, and _build_send_expedition_section returns null when idle == 0. Your first screenshot has Idle 0 and one party out, the second Idle 16 and none out — so each shows exactly one of the two, and they read as one block appearing and disappearing.

✳

The bottom-dock reshuffle and the flicker are symptoms of the same flat list

Because the panel is one undifferentiated stack of blocks, the wide dock has to guess at columns: _pack_wide_columns greedily assigns each block to whichever column is currently shortest, so a section's home changes whenever any height changes. Give the panel real zones and the guess disappears — see §05.

02

Three kinds of thing, and a fixed budget of pixels

Every control in the panel belongs to one of three subjects. The fix is to stop interleaving them — and then to spend a fixed height on each, so nothing ever needs to scroll.

The band

One camp, one place, one set of orders

Name, stage, population, larder, morale — and the verbs that act on the whole band: Move, Clear all. Belongs at the top, with the identity it modifies.

The workforce

Sixteen workers, allocated

Every worker is somewhere: a forage patch, a herd, a standing role, or idle. Sources and roles spend the same pool, so they share one budget readout — but they earn different row shapes, because only one of them yields food.

The parties

Workers who already left

Detached, elsewhere on the map, on their own clock. Listing them and launching them are one subject, not two sections with confusable names.

Neither band verb survives the split

Sorting the controls by subject exposed something better than a new home for Move and Clear all: neither one is really a band verb, and the panel is better off without both.

Move belongs to a tile, not to the band. "Move" arms a targeting mode and then waits for you to click a destination — the decision is about a place, and the panel that describes a place is the Tile panel. Moving it there turns a verb-first flow (arm, then hunt for a tile) into a target-first one (look at a tile, decide to go). Out of scope here — the Tile panel is not this proposal's to change — but it comes off the Band panel either way.

Clear all was one button for two unrelated demolitions. Today cancel_order wipes sources and standing roles in a single click, from a spot where it reads as belonging to Warrior. Split by subject it becomes two scoped actions living in the sections they act on — Unassign all work in Work, Recall all parties in Parties — each behind that section's ⋯ menu with an inline confirm naming exactly what it will and won't touch.

With both gone the band command row goes too, and the identity row carries the whole band header on its own. That is worth about one more row of work board in each dock — the no-scroll budget's cheapest win in this pass.

Two bars, two questions

The Band tab now carries a People bar — children, working age, elders, with the dependency ratio — directly above the Workforce bar. Same shape, deliberately different palettes: People is muted earth and slate (who the band is, which you do not control), Workforce is the saturated signal palette (what they are doing, which you do). Reading them stacked answers the question the old Population 30 · Workers 16 line only asserted: why are only sixteen of thirty people working.

This needs no schema change — PopulationCohortState already carries children, working and elders, and population_to_dict in native/src/lib.rs simply never decoded them. That is the same decoder the subsystem docs already flag for having silently dropped appended fields several times; this is another one. Three decoder lines, plus the keys added to marker_field_guard's consumed set.

Removing one of something

A source's removal is the labelled Unassign in the inspector strip — deliberately not a hover ✕ on the row, which would sit a destructive control millimetres from the − stepper. A party has neither stepper nor inspector, so it carries its own always-visible recall on the row. Neither asks for confirmation: both are one click to undo. The two bulk actions do ask, because thirty-four unassignments are not.

Where the no-scroll rule bites

Only one thing in the panel is unbounded: worked sources. Everything else has a fixed maximum — six vitals rows, one chart, two role cards, a handful of parties. So the whole design reduces to one question: how do you show N sources in a fixed box without a scrollbar? Three moves, in order of how much they buy:

MoveSources visible at once
Today's three-line row ~76px each 4
One-line row, detail on demand 28px each 14
Give the work list the whole panel tabs in the narrow dock 18
Flow into columns where width allows bottom dock, 3 columns 18
Beyond that: page, don't scroll filter chips cut the set first ‹ 1 / 2 ›

Paging rather than scrolling is the deliberate call. A pager is a fixed control at a fixed place, so panel height stops depending on content — which is also what kills the reservation churn behind the map flicker. And the filter chips mean paging is the rare case: at thirty-four sources you are normally looking at "the four that need attention", not at all of them.

05

What this does to the two bugs

The wide dock currently has no notion of what belongs where, so _pack_wide_columns distributes blocks into whichever column is shortest at that moment. Column membership is therefore a function of measured heights, and the pack runs against heights that are not yet settled — which is why Send Expedition changes columns when you press +.

With three named zones the wide dock stops guessing: band is a fixed left column, work is the flexible middle, parties is a fixed right column. Nothing migrates, because nothing is being balanced.

The no-scroll rule then finishes the job. Because the work board pages instead of growing, the panel's reserved height is a constant per dock edge — so reservation_changed stops firing on content changes, and MapView.set_reserved_inset stops throwing away the terrain cache mid-interaction. The greedy packer, the deferred re-measure pass and the fit-to-content height all go away together.

Two things still want fixing on their own merits alongside this: the double render per click (_after_pending_change rebuilds the whole panel twice), and the free-and-recreate of container nodes each pass. Both are cheap once the zones are static.

06

Decisions I need from you

Is paging an acceptable price for no scrolling?

At thirty-four sources in the narrow dock you get eighteen per page, so two pages — or one, once you tap a filter chip. The alternative is a scroller, which shows all thirty-four in one gesture but makes panel height content-dependent again.

LeaningPager, because it is what makes the fixed-height guarantee real — and the chips mean you rarely reach page two.

Where should the tab / column threshold sit?

The prototype hard-switches on dock edge: side means tabs, bottom means columns. It could instead key on actual width, so a wide left dock (or a narrow window with the panel at the bottom) picks the right shell on its own.

LeaningKey on width, not edge — same rule, no special cases, and it survives a resizable dock later.

Which tab opens by default in the narrow dock?

Work is where the decisions are, and it is the tab that justifies the redesign. Band is what the panel opens on today. Opening on Work means the first thing you see on selecting a band is its economy rather than its larder.

LeaningWork, with the vitals summarised in the identity row so the larder is never fully hidden.

Does the food outlook chart survive?

In the narrow dock it now has room on the Band tab, at full size with its empty ~turn N marker intact. In the wide dock it competes with the work board for the left column, so the prototype shows a sparkline there.

LeaningFull chart when the zone has the height, sparkline when it does not — one widget, two sizes, same data.

How much lands in one PR?

Two slices in one arc:

  1. Zones, responsive shell, band command row, role cards, unified Parties section. Fixes findings 02, 03 and 04 plus both layout bugs, and is verifiable against the existing band_panel_preview frames.
  2. Work board: one-line rows, filter chips, pager, pinned inspector. Fixes finding 01. Needs new fixtures at thirty-plus sources either way.

Does cancel_order gain a scope, or does the client send N zeroes?

Splitting Clear all means the client needs "unassign every source, leave the roles" — which today's cancel_order cannot express, since it clears everything. Either the verb takes a scope argument, or the client emits one assign_labor … 0 per source.

LeaningA scope on the verb — thirty-four commands for one click is a lot of wire traffic and thirty-four chances to half-apply, and the sim should own "what does clearing mean".

Where does Move actually land on the Tile panel?

The Tile panel already carries the forage assign controls for the selected tile, so a Move band here action alongside them is a natural fit — but it needs a band picker once there is more than one band, exactly as the forage control has. Worth designing with the multi-band slice rather than bolting on now.
